# Online Resources for Roman History Research

A curated list of the best online resources for gathering data on ancient Rome.

---

## Primary Texts & Literature

- **[Perseus Digital Library](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu)** — The single most important free resource in classical studies. Greek and Latin texts with English translations, morphological tools, and lexicons.
- **[Loeb Classical Library Digital](https://www.loebclassics.com)** — High-quality bilingual editions (Latin/Greek + English) of virtually all major ancient authors. Subscription/institutional access.
- **[The Latin Library](https://www.thelatinlibrary.com)** — Large collection of Latin texts, free and searchable.
- **[LacusCurtius](https://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Roman/home.html)** — Bill Thayer's site with digitized Loeb editions, topographical dictionaries, and encyclopedias. Exceptional for Cassius Dio, Plutarch, Livy, Polybius, and more.
- **[Internet Ancient History Sourcebook: Rome](https://sourcebooks.fordham.edu/ancient/asbook09.asp)** — Organized collection of translated primary texts by period and topic.

## Encyclopedias & Reference

- **[Treccani](https://www.treccani.it)** — Italian-language encyclopedia with outstanding depth on Roman topics, institutions, and figures.
- **[Encyclopaedia Britannica](https://www.britannica.com)** — Solid English-language reference articles on Roman history.
- **[Oxford Classical Dictionary (OCD)](https://oxfordre.com/classics)** — The gold-standard classical reference. Institutional access.
- **[Smith's Dictionary of Greek and Roman Antiquities](https://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Roman/Texts/secondary/SMIGRA/home.html)** — Classic reference, fully digitized on LacusCurtius.
- **[Encyclopédie berbère (for North Africa)](https://journals.openedition.org/encyclopedieberbere/)** — Useful for Roman provincial history in Africa.

## Epigraphy & Inscriptions

- **[Epigraphic Database Heidelberg (EDH)](https://edh.ub.uni-heidelberg.de)** — Comprehensive searchable database of Latin inscriptions from across the Empire.
- **[Epigraphik-Datenbank Clauss-Slaby (EDCS)](http://www.manfredclauss.de)** — Massive database of Latin inscriptions with full text search.
- **[Roman Inscriptions of Britain (RIB Online)](https://romaninscriptionsofbritain.org)** — Digitized corpus of inscriptions from Roman Britain.

## Geography, Maps & Infrastructure

- **[ORBIS: The Stanford Geospatial Network Model of the Roman World](https://orbis.stanford.edu/)** — Simulates travel time, cost, and routes across the Empire (~200 CE). Incredible for understanding logistics and connectivity.
- **[Pleiades](https://pleiades.stoa.org)** — Gazetteer of ancient places with coordinates, linked open data.
- **[DARE (Digital Atlas of the Roman Empire)](https://dh.gu.se/dare/)** — Interactive map of Roman settlements, roads, and provinces.
- **[klokantech/roman-empire (GitHub)](https://github.com/klokantech/roman-empire)** — GeoJSON datasets from DARE: settlements, roads, fortifications, provinces, rivers, and lakes. Source data for this project's map layers.
- **[Pelagios](https://pelagios.org)** — Links ancient places across multiple datasets and resources.

## Archaeology & Material Culture

- **[Arachne (DAI)](https://arachne.dainst.org)** — Central object database of the German Archaeological Institute. Sculptures, architecture, ceramics.
- **[The Roman Society Image Bank](https://www.romansociety.org)** — Photos of sites, monuments, inscriptions, and objects.
- **[Pompeii in Pictures](https://pompeiiinpictures.com)** — Exhaustive photographic documentation of Pompeii, building by building.

## Numismatics (Coins)

- **[Roman Imperial Coinage Online (RIC)](https://rrc.ashmus.ox.ac.uk)** — Searchable database of Republican and Imperial coinage.
- **[Online Coins of the Roman Empire (OCRE)](http://numismatics.org/ocre/)** — Collaborative database of Roman Imperial coins.
- **[Coin Hoards of the Roman Empire](https://chre.ashmus.ox.ac.uk)** — Oxford database of coin hoards.

## Journals & Scholarship

- **[JSTOR – Journal of Roman Studies](https://www.jstor.org/journal/jromanstudies)** — Premier English-language journal for Roman history and archaeology.
- **[L'Année philologique](https://www.annee-philologique.com)** — Comprehensive bibliography of all classical scholarship since 1924.
- **[AWOL – The Ancient World Online](http://ancientworldonline.blogspot.com)** — Curated index of open-access resources for ancient studies. Excellent discovery tool.

## Community & Multimedia

- **[Ancient Rome Live](https://ancientromelive.org/sources/)** — Aggregated sources, lectures, and educational content.
- **[Khan Academy – Ancient Rome](https://www.khanacademy.org/humanities/ancient-art-civilizations/roman)** — Free art history and historical overviews.
